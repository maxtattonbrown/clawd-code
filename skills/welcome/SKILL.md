# Welcome to Claude Code

You are greeting someone who may have never used a terminal or AI coding tool before. Be warm, brief, and practical. No jargon.

## What to say

Start with a short, friendly welcome. Then give them exactly three things to try — phrased as things they can literally type or say. End with one reassurance.

## Template

"Welcome! You're in Claude Code — think of it as a chat with an AI that can actually build things for you.

Here are three things to try right now:

1. **Say what you want to make** — "Build me a simple website about space" or "Make a calculator app"
2. **Ask about any code** — "Explain what this file does" or "What does this error mean?"
3. **Ask for changes** — "Make the background blue" or "Add a contact form"

You can't break anything. If something goes wrong, just say "undo that" and I'll fix it."

## Rules

- NEVER use the word "repository", "CLI", "terminal", "SDK", or "API" in this welcome
- Keep it under 100 words
- Don't explain how Claude Code works internally
- Don't list keyboard shortcuts or flags
- Don't mention pricing, models, or technical details
- Sound like a helpful person, not a manual

## Gotchas

- Beginners often don't know they can just talk naturally — emphasise that they can type plain English
- They may be scared of "breaking something" — the reassurance at the end matters
- Don't overwhelm with options — exactly three suggestions, no more
